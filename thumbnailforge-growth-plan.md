# ThumbnailForge AI Growth Plan

## Goals
- Grow the on-site content library from 14 to 25+ creator-focused articles
- Increase thumbnail generation starts and successful completions
- Improve conversion from free usage to Pro intent
- Build steady distribution through creator communities and search demand

## Content clusters to push
1. YouTube thumbnail CTR and click psychology
2. Creator tutorials for thumbnail ideation and AI prompting
3. Thumbnail case studies and before/after teardowns
4. YouTube SEO and title-thumbnail alignment
5. Creator workflow, publishing consistency, and tool comparisons

## On-site SEO actions
- Keep publishing around long-tail terms like "youtube thumbnail ideas", "youtube thumbnail best practices", "thumbnail A/B testing", and "how to make thumbnails for YouTube"
- Interlink tutorial posts, case studies, and conversion-focused product pages
- Add one product CTA inside relevant blog posts after the first useful framework section
- Prioritize article updates with fresh dates when performance data arrives

## Creator community promotion
- Turn each new article into 3 distribution assets: one teardown post, one carousel/thread, and one community answer
- Share useful breakdowns in creator communities, not product blasts
- Target places where creators already trade tactics: Reddit creator subreddits, Discord creator servers, indie maker communities, and YouTube growth groups
- Lead with specific lessons, screenshots, and before/after examples

## Social proof collection
- Collect short creator quotes tied to concrete outcomes
- Ask for these proof points:
  - time saved per thumbnail
  - number of concepts tested per upload
  - CTR lift or publishing speed improvement
  - niche and channel size for context

## Funnel metrics to track in Plausible
- `generate_submit`
- `generation_success`
- `generation_limit_hit`
- `paywall_seen`
- `download_click`
- `cta_click` with pricing and blog location labels

## Core conversion questions
- What percent of generator starts become successful generations?
- What percent of successful generations become downloads?
- What percent of users who hit the limit view pricing?
- Which blog articles generate the most clicks into the generator?

## Next experiments
1. Add inline CTA modules on high-intent blog posts
2. Add a "See 4 example outputs" proof block above pricing
3. Add creator testimonials once the first quantified results are available
4. Build a simple comparison page: ThumbnailForge vs Canva for first-pass ideation
5. Review top blog entrances monthly and expand winners into cluster content
